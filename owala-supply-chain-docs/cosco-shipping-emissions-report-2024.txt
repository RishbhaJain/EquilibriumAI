COSCO SHIPPING LINES CO., LTD.
ENVIRONMENTAL SUSTAINABILITY REPORT — 2024 (EXCERPT)
Fleet Carbon Emissions & Efficiency Data

================================================================================
COMPANY OVERVIEW
================================================================================

COSCO SHIPPING Lines Co., Ltd. is a subsidiary of China COSCO SHIPPING
Corporation Limited. Headquartered in Shanghai, COSCO operates one of the
world's largest container shipping fleets.

Total Fleet (as of Dec 2024):
  Container vessels:            512
  Total capacity:               2.94 million TEU
  Active routes:                428 (covering 142 countries/regions)

TRANS-PACIFIC ROUTE SPECIFIC DATA (Asia → US West Coast)
================================================================================

Route: China (Ningbo/Shanghai) → Long Beach / Los Angeles
Service: CPX (China-Pacific Express) and associated loops
Typical distance: 5,600-5,800 nautical miles
Typical transit time: 12-16 days

Vessels commonly deployed on this route:
  - COSCO Shipping Orchid (19,100 TEU, 2018)
  - COSCO Shipping Peony (19,100 TEU, 2018)
  - COSCO Shipping Jasmine (19,100 TEU, 2018)
  - COSCO Shipping Universe (21,237 TEU, 2019)
  - COSCO Shipping Nebula (19,100 TEU, 2018)

Route-specific emission factors (2024 measured data):

For 19,100 TEU class vessels (slow steaming at 16-17 knots):
  Fuel consumption per voyage (one-way):
    ~3,800 tonnes VLSFO (Ningbo → Long Beach)
  CO2 per voyage:
    ~11,970 tonnes CO2 (at 3.151 kg CO2 / kg VLSFO)
  Average container utilization: 92%
  Estimated TEU-miles: ~100.3 million per voyage
    (17,572 TEU loaded × 5,700 nm)

  CO2 per TEU:           ~680 kg CO2 per TEU (one-way)
  CO2 per TEU-km:        ~64.4 g CO2 per TEU-km
                         (= 680,000 g / 10,556 km)

  For a single 40'HC container (= 2 TEU):
    ~1,360 kg CO2 per container (one-way, Ningbo → Long Beach)

  Weight-based allocation:
    Average cargo weight per TEU: ~10.5 tonnes
    CO2 per tonne of cargo: ~64.8 kg CO2 per tonne
    CO2 per tonne-km: ~6.1 g CO2 per tonne-km

For 21,237 TEU class vessels:
  Slightly more efficient due to larger scale:
  ~620 kg CO2 per TEU (one-way)
  ~58.7 g CO2 per TEU-km

================================================================================
SLOW STEAMING VS EXPRESS SERVICE
================================================================================

COSCO employs slow steaming (reduced speed) on most Trans-Pacific routes
to reduce fuel consumption and emissions:

Speed       | Fuel/day  | Transit Days | Total Fuel | CO2/TEU
------------|-----------|--------------|------------|--------
22 kts      | 320 t/day | 11 days      | 3,520 t    | 710 kg   (full speed)
18 kts      | 210 t/day | 13 days      | 2,730 t    | 608 kg   (moderate)
16 kts      | 165 t/day | 15 days      | 2,475 t    | 545 kg   (slow steam)
14 kts      | 120 t/day | 17 days      | 2,040 t    | 448 kg   (ultra slow)

Standard COSCO CPX service operates at 16-17 knots (slow steaming).

================================================================================
FUEL SPECIFICATIONS
================================================================================

Primary fuel: Very Low Sulphur Fuel Oil (VLSFO)
  Sulphur content:          ≤ 0.50% (IMO 2020 compliant)
  Density:                  ~0.96 kg/liter
  Energy content:           ~40.2 MJ/kg
  CO2 emission factor:      3.151 kg CO2 per kg fuel
                            (per IMO GHG Fourth Study, 2020)
  Well-to-wake factor:      3.49 kg CO2e per kg fuel
                            (including upstream fuel production)

Auxiliary engines:
  Fuel: Marine Gas Oil (MGO)
  Consumption: ~5-8 tonnes/day
  CO2 factor: 3.206 kg CO2 per kg MGO

================================================================================
EMISSION REDUCTION TARGETS
================================================================================

  2030 target:  40% reduction in carbon intensity vs 2008 baseline
  2040 target:  70% reduction
  2050 target:  Net zero emissions

  Current progress (2024): 32% reduction vs 2008 baseline

  Initiatives:
    - Slow steaming program (fleet-wide since 2015)
    - Hull and propeller optimization
    - Air lubrication systems (on select newbuilds)
    - LNG dual-fuel vessels (24 ordered, 8 delivered)
    - Shore power connections at select ports
    - Methanol-ready newbuilds under order (6 vessels)
